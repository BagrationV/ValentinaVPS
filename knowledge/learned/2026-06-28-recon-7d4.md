### Top 3 Findings

**1. Co-Failure Ceiling on Multi-Model Systems** ⭐
- Paper: arXiv:2606.27288, "When Does Combining Language Models Help?"
- Tested routing/voting/MoA across **67 frontier models from 21 providers**
- Key finding: accuracy gain is capped by β (co-failure rate) — the rate all models are wrong on the same query
- β observed: 0.052 on open-ended math (2.5× higher than Gaussian copula predicts), 0.079 on code
- GPQA-Diamond: β = 0.127 in free-response form (5-judge panel, κ = 0.73–0.92)
- "Combining models rarely beats the single best model without a strong query-level routing signal"
- **Valentina relevance:** Directly informs our multi-provider routing strategy. Beyond ~5 diverse models, returns diminish. Our current DeepSeek v4 + GLM-5.2 (pending) pairing may be optimal.

**2. Prompt Injection in LLM Resume Screening** 🔐
