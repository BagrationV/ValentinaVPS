## Finding 1: Capability Frontier — Benchmarks Miss 82% of Model Performance
- **arXiv:** 2606.26836
- **Core insight:** Standard single-model, single-run benchmarks systematically understate real LLM capabilities. The "Capability Frontier" (Pareto frontier over models + generations) shows SOTA accuracy matched at **85% cost reduction** via oracle routing.
- **21 models across 16 benchmarks** (coding, reasoning, medicine, factuality, instruction, agentic tasks)
- **Key stat:** Single-model correction = 54% error reduction; +single-run = 82% improvement
- **Implication for us:** Validates our model-routing intel. Multi-model routing beats single models — but the Co-Failure Ceiling paper (below) puts an upper bound on this gain.

## Finding 1b: Co-Failure Ceiling — Routing Gains Are Bounded
- **arXiv:** 2606.27288
- **Core insight:** Multi-model systems (routing, voting, MoA) are capped by β — the rate at which ALL models are wrong on the same query. Across 67 models from 21 providers.
- β on open-ended math: 0.052 (empirical) vs 0.023 (predicted) — 2.5× underpricing
- β on code: 0.079
- **Key lesson:** Gains come from models failing on DIFFERENT questions, not from adding more models. Heterogeneous ensembles > homogeneous Self-MoA.
