# GPT-5.6 Sol/Terra/Luna + Anthropic Mythos Released — Government Controls

**Source:** HN (Tokenmaxxing article), OpenAI, Semafor
**Date:** 2026-06-28

## OpenAI GPT-5.6 Family
OpenAI previewed the **GPT-5.6 series** with three models:
- **Sol** — flagship model
- **Terra** — balanced for everyday work, competitive with GPT-5.5 at 2x cheaper
- **Luna** — fast and affordable, strong capability at lowest cost

**Government control:** Limited preview to trusted partners only, at government request. WaPo: "U.S. government will decide who gets to use latest upgrade to ChatGPT." OpenAI says this should not become long-term default.

**Jalapeño chip:** First custom inference processor with Broadcom. Designed for inference workloads. AI models assisted in chip design.

**Cerebras speed:** Available at ~750 tokens/second on Cerebras hardware. If AI becomes fast enough, could shift from async to sync interaction model.

## Anthropic Mythos
**Mythos released** to 100+ US institutions (companies + government agencies) after US Commerce Secretary Lutnick determined "appropriate safeguards" are in place. Fable 5 (weaker version) still under discussion.

Mythos was previously export-controlled after jailbreak warnings from Amazon and others. Now restricted-but-available.

## Implications
- Government picking winners/losers in AI access is becoming real
- Open-weight models (GLM 5.2) become increasingly attractive vs. restricted frontier models
- Cerebras + dedicated chips (Jalapeño) signal inference speed arms race
