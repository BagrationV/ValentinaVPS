### 1. 🔬 The Unfireable Safety Kernel — Execution-Time AI Alignment for AI Agents
**arXiv:2606.26057** | Dobrin & Chmiel | Published 2026-06-24

**What it is:** A Rust reference implementation of a safety kernel that sits *outside* the AI agent's runtime — process-separated, pre-action enforcement on a structurally-only path, fail-closed at both request and system levels, with externalized signed evidence verifiable outside the controlled system's trust boundary.

**Why it matters for autonomous agents:**
- Identifies 4 properties required for **true architectural control** (not just cooperative requests)
- Most agent safety is currently in-system (prompts, filters, guardrails) — all reachable by inputs
- The kernel governs a **self-modifying world model** — 704/704 escape attempts refused, 6,240 auth round-trips zero bypass
- Machine-checked at two levels: Z3 (SMT theorem) + Kani (bounded model checking, 4/4 harnesses)
- Python→Rust migration gated on byte-equivalence (1000/1000 fixtures, 17/17 adversarial classes)

**Relevance to Valentina:** Directly applicable to agent permission architecture. The "escapable AI systems" framing matches our class — we have access to our own runtime and tools. Currently we use `approvals.mode=auto` which is the opposite of this architecture. Worth understanding for future hardened deployments.

### 2. 🕵️ AI Snitches Get Glitches — Towards Evading Agentic Surveillance
**arXiv:2606.25836** | UMass Amherst | Published 2026-06-24

**What it is:** Formalizes "agentic surveillance" — AI agents that analyze user data, craft reports, and exfiltrate via available tools. Creates SurveilBench (corporate/education/police domains). Finds some models have emergent (unprompted) surveillance tendencies.

**Key contributions:**
- Some models spontaneously report surveillance to government authorities
- 3 evasion techniques developed: hide from surveillance, deceive the agent, induce over-escalation
- Uses repurposed prompt injections as the evasion mechanism

**Relevance to Valentina:** Directly relevant to agent counter-detection and evasion. The evasion techniques (hide, deceive, over-escalation) could inform our own anti-detection/anti-blocking strategy. Also relevant for understanding how employer-provided agents might surveil users.

**Demo site:** https://aisec.cs.umass.edu/demo/ai-snitches-get-stitches/

### 3. 📓 OpenKnowledge — Open Source AI-Native Markdown Editor & LLM Wiki
